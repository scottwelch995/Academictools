Classroom Environment, Opening & Closing
Does the room set students up for learning — and does the teacher begin and end class with intention?
CTEs Classroom Culture — Physical Environment · Engagement · Instructional Practice — Planning & Preparation
Dispositions Conscientiousness · Consistency · Gravitas
Seven Laws Law 1 — The Law of the Teacher: the teacher must be thoroughly prepared · Law 7 — The Law of Review and Application: learning is consolidated through review at the opening and closing of class
Christian Faith visible in daily classroom rhythms — prayer, Scripture, and devotion as the foundation of the school day, not an afterthought
Observer Focus
A well-managed classroom begins before the teacher speaks. The room itself communicates expectations. How class opens and closes reveals whether the teacher is leading with intention — or reacting.
Specific Look-Fors
▸Does the room's arrangement promote attention and focus — or invite distraction?
▸Is technology serving the lesson — or doing the lesson for the teacher?
▸Did class begin with faith, intention, and a task — or did it drift into existence?
▸Did class end with review and intention — or did the bell dismiss the students?

Physical Environment

Observable any time regardless of when you arrive. These items reflect choices the teacher made before students walked in.

The seating arrangement promotes attention and engagement
Consider: can students see the teacher and board clearly? Does the arrangement encourage focus — or make side conversation easy and natural?
Students have an assigned seating chart that appears intentional
Examples: students go directly to assigned seats, the arrangement appears purposeful rather than student-chosen
The room is orderly and prepared — it communicates that learning is serious and valued here
Examples: materials organized, board is intentional and current, the room feels like a place where important work happens
Use of Technology

The question is not whether technology was used — but whether it served the learning, or shifted intellectual work away from students and onto a screen.

Teacher use of technology supported rather than replaced direct instruction
Supporting: a map displayed for reference, a primary source projected for discussion. Replacing: reading slides aloud word for word, playing a video as the primary lesson, navigating away from content while students wait
The technology used was the most effective tool for this moment
A smartboard showing notes students passively read would be more effective as a handout. Slides students copy would be more engaging built live on the whiteboard with student input. Ask: did students engage with content — or watch a screen?
Class Opening

How a teacher opens class reveals whether they are leading with intention — and whether their faith is visible in the daily rhythm of their classroom.

Teacher greeted students at the door or as they entered
Examples: standing at the door, making eye contact, using student names, noticing the mood or energy students brought with them
Class began with prayer, Bible reading, or a devotional opening
Examples: teacher or student led prayer, a Scripture passage read aloud, a brief devotional — the faith foundation of the school day was made visible in this classroom
Teacher had something for students to do as they arrived
Examples: a warm-up on the board, a question to think about, materials to retrieve and begin — students were productive from the moment they sat down
Teacher read the room as students arrived — noticing energy, attitudes, and who needed attention
Examples: adjusted approach based on what they observed, noticed a student who seemed off, greeted a student warmly who looked disengaged
Teacher connected the opening to prior learning — a brief review before new content began
Examples: warm-up question referencing yesterday's lesson, a quick oral review, asking students to recall a key concept — Law 7: learning is consolidated through review
Class Closing

How a teacher closes class reveals whether the lesson had a destination — and whether the teacher or the bell is in charge of the room.

Teacher used the closing to review or consolidate what was learned
Examples: asked students to summarize the key idea, gave a brief review question, connected today's lesson to what comes next — Law 7: the closing is the final opportunity to seal what was taught
Homework or closing assignment was communicated clearly and appeared prepared in advance
Prepared: written on the board, explained clearly, students knew exactly what was required. Improvised: teacher appeared to create it on the spot, instructions were vague or changed mid-explanation
Class ended with intention — a closing prayer, ritual, or clear signal from the teacher
Examples: closing prayer, a moment of reflection, a consistent dismissal signal — the teacher dismissed the class, not the bell
⚠ The bell dismissed the class — not the teacher
Students began packing up or moving before the teacher gave a dismissal signal — the teacher lost the closing moment to the clock
